benjamin harrison nominated for president on the eighth ballot at the 1888 republican convention benjamin harrison conducted one of the first front-porch campaign delivering short speech to delegation that visited him in indianapolis as he wa only 5 foot 6 inch tall democrats called him little ben ; republicans replied that he wa big enough to wear the hat of his grandfather old tippecanoe born in 1833 on a farm by the ohio river below cincinnati harrison attended miami university in ohio and read law in cincinnati he moved to indianapolis where he practiced law and campaigned for the republican party he married caroline lavinia scott in 1853 after the civil war he wa colonel of the 70th volunteer infantry harrison became a pillar of indianapolis enhancing his reputation a a brilliant lawyer the democrats defeated him for governor of indiana in 1876 by unfairly stigmatizing him a kid gloves harrison in the 1880's he served in the united states senate where he championed indians homesteader and civil war veteran in the presidential election harrison received 100 000 fewer popular vote than cleveland but carried the electoral college 233 to 168 although harrison had made no political bargain his supporter had given innumerable pledge upon his behalf when boss matt quay of pennsylvania heard that harrison ascribed his narrow victory to providence quay exclaimed that harrison would never know how close a number of men were compelled to approach the penitentiary to make him president harrison wa proud of the vigorous foreign policy which he helped shape the first pan american congress met in washington in 1889 establishing an information center which later became the pan american union at the end of his administration harrison submitted to the senate a treaty to annex hawaii; to his disappointment president cleveland later withdrew it substantial appropriation bill were signed by harrison for internal improvement naval expansion and subsidy for steamship line for the first time except in war congress appropriated a billion dollar when critic attacked the billion-dollar congress speaker thomas b reed replied this is a billion-dollar country president harrison also signed the sherman anti-trust act to protect trade and commerce against unlawful restraint and monopoly the first federal act attempting to regulate trust the most perplexing domestic problem harrison faced wa the tariff issue the high tariff rate in effect had created a surplus of money in the treasury low-tariff advocate argued that the surplus wa hurting business republican leader in congress successfully met the challenge representative william mckinley and senator nelson w aldrich framed a still higher tariff bill; some rate were intentionally prohibitive harrison tried to make the tariff more acceptable by writing in reciprocity provision to cope with the treasury surplus the tariff wa removed from imported raw sugar; sugar grower within the united states were given two cent a pound bounty on their production long before the end of the harrison administration the treasury surplus had evaporated and prosperity seemed about to disappear a well congressional election in 1890 went stingingly against the republicans and party leader decided to abandon president harrison although he had cooperated with congress on party legislation nevertheless his party renominated him in 1892 but he wa defeated by cleveland after he left office harrison returned to indianapolis and married the widowed mrs mary dimmick in 1896 a dignified elder statesman he died in 1901
